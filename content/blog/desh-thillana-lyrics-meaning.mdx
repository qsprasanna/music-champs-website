## What is Desh Thillana?

Desh Thillana is not a single word but a combination of two — **Desh** and **Thillana**.

- **Desh** is a raag — a melodic framework used in both Hindustani and Carnatic classical music
- **Thillana** is a type of musical composition in **Carnatic music**

A Thillana is similar to a **Tarana** in Hindustani classical music — both are fast-paced and built primarily from rhythmic, meaningless syllables like _ta na dhim dere_, _jham jham jhampa_, and _thom thom_ rather than lyrical words. Thillanas are typically performed as the grand concluding piece in classical concerts and **Bharatanatyam** recitals.

## Structure of a Thillana

A Thillana follows a specific compositional structure:

| Section        | Role                                                        |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Pallavi**    | Opening theme — sets the melodic and rhythmic identity      |
| **Anupallavi** | Second section that develops the theme                      |
| **Charanam**   | The body — may include meaningful words                     |
| **Refrain**    | Rhythmic outburst of syllables — the most energetic section |

## Full Lyrics — Desh Thillana

### Verse 1 — Pallavi

> Desh raagam, thillana gati,
> Thaka dhimi thaka dhimi thaana.

### Verse 2 — Pallavi

> Nadhroopaka, nadhopaka,
> Thaka dhimi thaka dhimi thaana.

### Verse 3 — Charanam

> Sarasamukhi, mrudhu bhashini,
> Madhura koojitha sangeetha priya.
>
> Sarasa nadha sudha rasika,
> Sarasa padha nidha paksha vahini.

### Refrain

> Thara jham jham jhampa nidham nidham,
> Thom thom thom thom thom thom thom thom.

### Outro — Pallavi

> Desh raagam, thillana gati,
> Thaka dhimi thaka dhimi thaana.
