## Introduction

Indian Classical music is one of the oldest forms of music that exists among the world's wide range of classical music forms. It is music based on raagas, dwanis, swaras and taals. There are many raagas in Indian Classical music and one such raaga is **Raag Bhairavi**. Let us understand in detail about the swaras of Raag Bhairavi.

## Raag Bhairavi Parichay

| Feature         | Detail                             |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Swar**        | RE, GA, DHA, NI Komal — MA Shuddha |
| **Jati**        | Sampoorn-Sampoorn                  |
| **Thaat**       | Bhairavi                           |
| **Vaadi**       | MA (Madhyam)                       |
| **Samvaadi**    | SA (Shadaj)                        |
| **Gayan Samay** | Pratah Kaal Pratham Prahar         |
| **Aaroh**       | SA RE GA MA PA DHA NI SA'          |
| **Avroh**       | SA' NI DHA PA MA GA RE SA          |

## Notes of Raag Bhairavi

Raag Bhairavi consists of both shuddha notes and komal notes. **Shuddha notes** are the notes that are in the original form of existence and **komal notes** are the flattened form of shuddha notes.

There are 4 komal and one shuddha swara in Raag Bhairavi. The swaras **re, ga, dha** and **ni** are komal whereas the **ma** is shuddha. Sa and pa are achal — meaning they can neither be komal nor shuddha.

**Aaroh:** sa re(k) ga(k) ma pa dha(k) ni(k) Sa'

**Avroh:** sa' — ni — dha — pa — ma — ga — re — sa
